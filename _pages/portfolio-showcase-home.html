Understand (20%)

  • Identify the problem your clients are facing
  • What problem are they trying to solve?
  • Who are their target audience and users?
  • Talk to your clients users and understand them
  • Find patterns

Research & Test (70%)

  • Find out what competitors are doing well and not so well
  • Identify the ideal user journey flow for each target audience/user
  • Workshop - depending on the information found
  • Start rough sketches and constantly re-iterate them from user testing
  • Create wireframes (clickable) and constantly re-iterate them from user testing - ensure each screen aligns to the journey
  • Nail the user experience

Design (15%)

  • Create a design system and ensure the user interface is accessible
  • High-fidelity screens and a clickable prototype
  • User test and make minor adjustments and fixes accordingly

Considerations

  • MVP - what are the key requirements for a product and where to spend significant amount of time
  • Technical complexities - what is feasible and reasonable
  • Starting small and consistently scoping is ideal, there will always be opportunities for better solutions down the line

  • Accessibility - we want everyone to feel included even though it might not align with the brand
  • Strictly spending time on a design system will prevent inconsistencies in the future
  • We're not always trying
